about me

My name is Yi Xu, majoring in Physics, now a senior undergraduate student from Department of Physics and Chu Kochen Honors College at Zhejiang University.

My research interests lie within the broad area of condensed matter physics, including fluctuation-caused phenomena in biophysics like phase seperation in some biological systems, non-equilibrium statistical physics and many other areas of condensed matter physics. I took various courses during the past three years, including quantum mechanics, statistical physics, solid state physics and so on, to prepare for my future research. Now I have obtained a GPA of 3.94/4.00 (or 90.67/100) and rank 2 out of 19 in the Qiushi Class co-established by Department of Physics and Chu Kochen Honors College, which selects top students majoring in physics and requires more advanced courses.

Recently, I am learning group theory and, at the same time, actively working on a project that focuses on using MD simulation to study the mechanism of conformational fluctuation of disordered proteins, a type of proteins which can cause phase seperation phenomena in some biological systems, which is supervised by Professsor Jingyuan Li, my current advisor in Zhejiang University. During the summer of 2019, I had the oppoturnity to work in Professor Gustavo E. Scuseria‘s group (http://scuseria.rice.edu/) at Department of Physics and Astronomy at Rice University and was supervised by Gaurav Harsha. My topic was the implementation of thermal coupled cluster theory for Lipkin model, which is based on some of the group’s previous works ([1][2][3]) and will be expanded into other spin systems in the future.

I am now looking for a PhD position and will enroll in 2020 fall. Look forward to joining your group!
